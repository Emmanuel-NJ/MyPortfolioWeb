About Me

Emmanuel Njobvu

“As a Bachelor’s degree ICT student at Chalimbana University, I am passionate about exploring cybersecurity, software development, and UX/UI design to create meaningful, industry-focused solutions. Under the guidance of dedicated mentors, I am training to become a teacher, learning how to inspire and educate future tech enthusiasts. I am eagerly looking forward to working on a project that can bring significant impact to Zambian communities, particularly in industrial and local contexts.”

“I am actively developing my skills in penetration testing, network security, and secure software development, aiming to build safer and more reliable systems. My focus is on creating user-friendly interfaces and innovative technologies that address local challenges, especially in agriculture and finance, as I continue to enhance my abilities in design and functionality for industrial applications.”

“Beyond my studies, I am a proud member of the Information and Communications Technology Association of Zambia (ICTAZ), which connects ICT students and professionals, providing me with opportunities to learn from industry leaders. I mentor peers in cybersecurity, participate in hackathons to refine my skills, and draw from my teaching mentorship to share knowledge. My goal is to combine security, functionality, and design to empower Zambia’s industries and communities through technology, while contributing to the growth of the tech ecosystem as an aspiring educator.”
